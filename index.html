Day 02 — Full Day
South Mumbai,
the real thing.
Leave by 9:30 AM · Beat the heat and the crowds
"The city's historic core. Give it a full day."
9:30
AM
Jehangir Art Gallery Culture
Free entry, 20–30 minutes. Whatever's showing. Kala Ghoda is worth wandering regardless — the whole neighbourhood has good energy in the morning.
⏱ 30 min
10:00
AM
Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya Museum
Formerly the Prince of Wales Museum. Genuinely one of the better city museums in India — across natural history, Indian art, and arms. Don't skip it.
⏱ 2 hours
12:00
PM
Kala Ghoda Cafe Coffee
Right there. Small, good coffee, nice pause before the afternoon push south.
⏱ 20 min
1:00
PM
Gateway of India Sight
It's touristy but it's the real thing. Stand at the waterfront, take it in. The scale works in person. The harbour view from here is the Mumbai postcard.
⏱ 30 min
1:30
PM
Bade Miyan Lunch
5 minutes from the Gateway. Order the seekh rolls. Eat standing on the street. This is the move.
⏱ 45 min
◆ 2:30 PM — Pick your afternoon
Recommended
Elephanta Caves
Ferry from Gateway (~1 hr each way, 1 hr at the caves). UNESCO site. Rock-cut temples, 6th century. The boat ride is part of it. Last ferry back ~5:30 PM.
Slower Afternoon
Cafe Veronica's
Stay in Colaba. Browse the market, sit here for cold coffee. A very specific old-Mumbai atmosphere that hasn't changed in 40 years.
6:00
PM
Haji Ali Dargah Sight
Check tide times before going — the causeway floods at high tide. Island mosque, causeway, sea on both sides. The setting does most of the work.
⏱ 45 min
7:30
PM
Bandra-Worli Sea Link On the way
Ask your cab to take the Sealink back to Bandra. 3 minutes to cross. Worth it once, especially at dusk — the cable-stayed bridge is impressive from the car.
⏱ In transit
Dinner — pick your mood
Classic Mumbai
Sardar Pav Bhaji
Tardeo, near Haji Ali and on the way back. The original. Worth the stop.
Light + Coffee
Subko Cacao, Bandra
Back in Bandra. Lighter option with excellent chocolate and coffee.
⚑
Haji Ali tide note: check tide-forecast.com for Mahim Bay before going. Causeway closes at high tide — aim for 1–2 hrs before or after high tide.

Day 03 — Full Day
Bandra, Temple
& Cinema.
Late start · 10:30 AM · No rush this morning
"Shopping, a temple, and slow afternoon energy."
10:30
AM
Linking Road Shopping
Clothes, accessories, good for browsing and bargaining. Go early before it gets crowded. Part of a 2–3 hr shopping block across three streets.
⏱ Part of 2–3 hr block
11:30
AM
Hill Road Shopping
Denser, more chaotic than Linking Road. Better for random finds. Elco Pani Puri is here if you skipped it on Day 1.
⏱ Part of 2–3 hr block
12:15
PM
Chapel Road Thrift Stores Shopping
Vintage and secondhand — small shops, hit or miss, but the right kind of interesting. A loop through takes 30–45 mins and costs nothing if you don't buy anything.
⏱ 30–45 min
◆ 1:00 PM — Lunch options
Worth the trip
Papa Pancho Da Dhaba
Vile Parle — 20 min auto from Bandra. Proper Punjabi. Best dal makhani in the city. Worth the trip.
Stay local
Nandan / Mercii Pub Golf
Nandan Coffee if you want something light; Mercii Pub Golf for afternoon drinks and food. Both in Bandra.
3:00
PM
Siddhivinayak Temple Temple
Prabhadevi, 20 min from Bandra. One of Mumbai's most visited — Ganesh deity, ornate interiors, serious devotional energy. Weekday afternoons have shorter queues.
⏱ 1–1.5 hours
5:00
PM
National Museum of Indian Cinema Museum
Films Division campus, Pedder Road. A proper curated museum — not a theme park. Genuinely underrated. If you have any interest in Bollywood history, this is the right version of it.
⏱ 1.5 hours
Evening — cafe or drinks in Bandra
Coffee
Nandan Coffee
Slow afternoon in Bandra. Good if you want to decompress after the temple.
Chocolate + Coffee
Subko Cacao
Excellent cacao drinks and pastries. A treat after a long day on your feet.

Day 04 — Morning & Afternoon
Film City
& out.
Leave Bandra 8:30 AM · Back by mid-afternoon
"Bollywood's backyard. Then lunch and you're done."
8:30
AM
Depart Bandra Transit
~45 minutes to Goregaon depending on traffic. Book the Film City tour in advance — slots fill up. Government-run tours available through Maharashtra Tourism.
⏱ 45 min drive
9:30
AM
Film City, Goregaon Experience
Backlot sets, active shoots, the sheer scale of the place. Book in advance. Genuinely fun even with only passing interest in Hindi cinema. They shoot everything here — period streets, village sets, corporate offices, jungles.
⏱ 3–4 hours
1:30
PM
Papa Pancho Da Dhaba, Vile Parle Lunch
Between Film City and Bandra on the way back. The correct final meal — dal makhani, butter chicken, laccha paratha. Eat well before the flight.
⏱ 1 hour
3:00
PM
Back in Bandra Wind Down
Pack, debrief. Buffer time for anything you want to revisit — or one final Bombay Sweet Shop run for mithai to take home. Karachi halwa, mawa cake, milk barfi.
⏱ Rest of day
⚑
Book Film City ahead: Maharashtra Tourism runs guided tours, or book via their official site. Go at least a day before — weekend slots especially fill fast.
